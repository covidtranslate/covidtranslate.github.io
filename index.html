COVID-19 Screening Center Guidelines

KCDC drive-thru manual

New

This document from KCDC covers the basic operating components of a drive-thru screening center. These screening centers have five "stations"—appointment, registration, screening, specimen collection, and patient disinfection and instruction—that can be set up in one of two different ways, depending on each agency's situation. KCDC COVID-19 Response Guidelines translated into more languages

Today, we're excited to announce the next major milestone in our mission to spread knowledge worldwide to fight COVID-19! Since the release of the first draft ("v0.9") of the KCDC COVID-19 Response Guidelines (the "playbook"), volunteers from around the world took the initiative even further and started translating into their own native languages. COVID-19 Screening Center Guidelines

Drive-thru screening center operation manual in Sejong City

This one-page manual describes the operational steps of drive-thru screening centers installed throughout the city of Sejong, South Korea's administrative capital with a population of 280,000. It lays out in detail the roles of each station at the drive-thru site as well as guidance and instructions for site administrators from pre-screening preparations to post-screening decontamination.

COVID-19 Response Guidelines

KCDC COVID-19 Response Guidelines translation v1.0 released

Today, we're releasing the English version 1.0 of Korea CDC’s COVID-19 playbook for local governments, complete with appendices. This is the amazing work of global volunteers working around the clock since March 28, 2020. This playbook contains detailed, updated protocols for healthcare providers, government officials and ordinary citizens regarding quarantine, treatment, disinfection, funeral arrangements, and more from both a policy and a patient care perspective. We hope making this document available to a broader audience will save lives and help fight this pandemic.

Contact tracing strategy in South Korea

Contact tracing has been a key component of South Korea's strategy to fight COVID-19.
